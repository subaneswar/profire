Fire Detection and Alarm

OUTPUT

Components

Components:

  1. Arduino Uno
  2. Flame Sensor
  3. Buzzer
  4. LED
  5. Resistor (220-330 ohms)
  6. Breadboard and wires(alway use Breadboard because it will align)

Steps:

  1. Place the Arduino Uno and a breadboard on the Tinkercad canvas

  2. Connect the positive (longer) leg of the LED to a digital pin (e.g., D2) through a current-limiting resistor (220-330 ohms).
    Connect the negative (shorter) leg of the LED to the ground (GND) pin on the Arduino.

  3. Connect one leg of the buzzer to another digital pin (e.g., D3).
    Connect the other leg of the buzzer to the ground (GND) pin on the Arduino.

  4. Place the flame sensor on the breadboard. It usually has three pins: VCC, GND, and OUT.

  5. Connect the VCC pin of the flame sensor to the 5V output on the Arduino.

  6. Connect the GND pin of the flame sensor to the ground (GND) pin on the Arduino.

  7. Connect the OUT pin of the flame sensor to an analog pin (e.g., A0) on the Arduino.

  8. Write the Arduino code to read the flame sensor's analog input, detect a flame, and trigger the LED and buzzer.
